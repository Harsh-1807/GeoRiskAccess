Hardware Components

Our Sensor Array

Our Real Time Weather system utilizes a sophisticated array of sensors and components to provide accurate and comprehensive environmental data. Here's a breakdown of the key hardware elements:

  • DHT Sensor: Measures temperature and humidity with high precision.
  • RTC Module: Records real-time data with an accurate timestamp, ensuring our measurements are properly logged and organized.
  • MQ135 Sensors: Monitor various gases including CO, NO2, and SO2, providing crucial air quality information.
  • Sharp Optical Dust Sensor (GP2Y1014AU0F): Detects fine particulate matter (PM2.5), a key indicator of air pollution levels.
  • Microcontroller: We utilize a powerful microcontroller (such as Arduino or Raspberry Pi) to interface with all sensor modules, process the data, and log it efficiently.

System Integration

Our team has carefully integrated these components to create a robust and reliable environmental monitoring system. The microcontroller serves as the brain of the operation, coordinating data collection from all sensors and ensuring accurate, time-stamped readings.

This hardware setup allows us to provide you with comprehensive, real-time environmental data, helping you stay informed about the air quality and weather conditions in your area.
